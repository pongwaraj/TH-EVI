## Proposal Notes

### MeeChok Plaza, Chiang Mai

MeeChok Plaza is a strong Fa Ham/Ruamchok community retail node, but the surrounding charging market is very competitive. The main nearby threats are Green Park/Fair Super Charge, EA Anywhere My Hip, Tesla Big C Extra, and Meta Mall. A 4-port layout is the conservative payback case, but a 6-port distributed 720 kW class station is better if the project wants to use port count and speed as a demand magnet.

Base proposal number: 55 sessions/day, about 1,540 kWh/day, about 10,500 THB/day gross.

### Sunee Grand Hotel, Ubon Ratchathani

Sunee Grand is an inner-city hotel, mall, and convention destination near the airport-side core. It has stronger destination dwell time than a pure roadside stop, but it faces competition from EA Anywhere Ubon Square, PTT/EV Station PluZ, Central Ubon/Tesla, and PEA VOLTA candidates. The recommended proposal stance is 6 ports distributed if positioning matters, with 4 ports as a payback-sensitive option.

Base proposal number: 50 sessions/day, about 1,400 kWh/day, about 9,500 THB/day gross.

### Fairy Plaza, Khon Kaen

Fairy Plaza has good downtown demand near Kaen Nakhon, the railway station, Ton Tann, and Central Khon Kaen. The risk is older mall format and unverified parking. It should not be treated as a highway hub because Route 2/Mittraphap chargers will capture much of the through-traffic.

Base proposal number: 48 sessions/day, about 1,344 kWh/day, about 9,100 THB/day gross.

### Centosa Central Branch, Khon Kaen

The Glang Muang branch should be on hold. The map heat is good, but dedicated parking has not been verified. If it has no private lot or only street parking, it should be removed from the DC pipeline. The earlier 52 sessions/day concept is suspended until parking is proven.

Proposal status: hold / need field verification.

### Centosa Sila Branch, Khon Kaen

Centosa Sila is the preferred Centosa candidate for field verification because it is more likely to have usable parking than the old downtown branch. It is still screening-only until exact parking count, frontage, transformer space, and operating hours are confirmed.

Screening number: 42 sessions/day, about 1,176 kWh/day, about 8,000 THB/day gross.

### Tang Ngee Soon Superstore Nadi, Udon Thani

This site is a west-side supermarket/neighborhood-roadside node, not a UD Town-level urban hub. It is suitable for local repeat use and airport/west ring-road catchment. A 4-port 180 kW x2 station fits the base case; 6 ports would be too heavy unless field traffic is much stronger than expected.

Base proposal number: 38 sessions/day, about 1,064 kWh/day, about 7,200 THB/day gross.

### Asawann Shopping Complex 1, Nong Khai

Asawann is one of the strongest Nong Khai candidates because it combines city retail with Thai-Lao Friendship Bridge gateway demand and has public evidence of a front parking area. It should still be discounted for the smaller Nong Khai market and possible Route 2/border chargers.

Base proposal number: 42 sessions/day, about 1,260 kWh/day, about 8,600 THB/day gross.

### V Viang Lifestyle Mall, Nong Khai

V Viang is a city lifestyle and riverfront demand site near the bus terminal, Tha Sadet, hospital, and city center. It is less gateway-oriented than Asawann, but better for city, tourist, and restaurant dwell-time demand. Parking appears positive from public review sources.

Base proposal number: 42 sessions/day, about 1,176 kWh/day, about 8,000 THB/day gross.

### U Park Ubon

U Park is a strong north-side Chayangkun lifestyle/roadside destination. It has parking evidence and is better than Sunee for north-side roadside positioning, but it is close to EA Anywhere Ubon Square and must be heavily competition-adjusted. A 6-port distributed layout is justified because 4 ports may create queue risk at the base case.

Base proposal number: 58 sessions/day, about 1,624 kWh/day, about 11,000 THB/day gross.

### Charoenphan Phayao

Charoenphan is a small-city destination site near Kwan Phayao, Phayao Hospital, the city center, and bus terminal. It has parking evidence, but Phayao has a smaller EV demand pool, and Route 1 chargers can pull highway traffic away. It should be proposed as a conservative city-destination/coverage station, not a high-throughput hub.

Base proposal number: 30 sessions/day, about 840 kWh/day, about 5,700 THB/day gross.

### UD Town, Udon Thani

UD Town remains one of the strongest Udon urban lifestyle and transport-core candidates. It can support a 6-port distributed proposal if parking and access are confirmed. Avoid presenting more than 90 sessions/day without new field evidence.

Base proposal number: 60 sessions/day, about 1,680 kWh/day, about 11,400 THB/day gross.

### Mahachok Park, Chiang Mai

Mahachok Park fits the 4-port 180 kW x2 format. It is a community/roadside destination on the east side of Chiang Mai, not a major hub. Upside depends on Route 118 capture, frontage, signage, and parking quality.

Base proposal number: 38 sessions/day, about 1,064 kWh/day, about 7,200 THB/day gross.

### Punn Suk San Kamphaeng, Chiang Mai

Punn Suk should be handled conservatively because current site readiness appears weaker than Mahachok. It may work as a slower-ramp east-side destination if the investor accepts lower utilization in the first year.

Base proposal number: 25 sessions/day, about 700 kWh/day, about 4,800 THB/day gross.

### Central Airport Chiang Mai

Central Airport is the clear large-format hot-zone case. The site has user-confirmed surface parking redevelopment and sits in the airport/Central Airport super hot zone. The ground-truth anchor is the Super EV Hub at around 130 sessions/day typical and about 300 sessions/day peak high season. A 12-port 720 kW distributed type is justified.

Base proposal number: 150 sessions/day, about 4,200 kWh/day, about 28,500 THB/day gross.

## Field Verification Checklist

For every investor-facing site, verify these before final CAPEX approval:

1. Dedicated parking capacity and number of EV bays that can be reserved.
2. Entry/exit path and whether charging cars block retail operations.
3. Visibility from road and signage options.
4. Transformer/cabinet/dispenser placement.
5. Real nearby DC competitors in operator apps: PEA VOLTA, EV Station PluZ, EA Anywhere, EleXA, Evolt, Tesla, MG.
6. Operating hours and whether charging can remain open after mall closing.
7. Whether 4-port, 6-port, or 12-port layout matches first-year sessions per port.
